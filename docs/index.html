Live on Azure — Formula 1 sources flowing end-to-end

Every open motorsport job, in one feed.

Motorsport teams and their technology partners run dozens of different careers portals on a dozen different platforms. Paddock Jobs collects, normalizes, and de-duplicates every posting into a single searchable feed — with saved searches and email alerts, so you never miss the one that matters. Formula 1 is fully covered today; WEC/IMSA endurance racing is next.

Why this exists

I'm a senior SRE/cloud engineer and Navy veteran working to break into motorsport — and tired of manually checking a dozen different careers portals for openings. This is both a tool I use myself and a demonstration of the cloud architecture, data engineering, and product instincts I'd bring to a technology role in the paddock.

The platform never takes applications directly — every listing links straight back to the team's own official posting.

What's built

Seven sources flowing end-to-end today: collector → normalize/dedupe/diff → Azure Cosmos DB → API → web feed. 102 unit tests across 7 workspaces, plus full account/search flows verified in a real browser against a mock API.

Unified, searchable feed

Filter by team, category, workplace type, and employment type, or full-text search across title and description.

Source health monitoring

Every collector run is logged — postings found, new, closed, and errors — with a public health indicator per source.

Accounts & alerts

Passwordless (magic-link) sign-in. Save a search and get a daily or weekly email digest the moment something new matches.

Hiring trends

Postings-opened per team over a configurable window, derived entirely from collector history — no extra data collection required.

"New this week"

A shareable, no-login digest page grouped by team — built for organic sharing in motorsport communities.

Pluggable collectors

Every source implements one small interface. Adding a team is a config file, not a core-code change.

Coverage

7 of 11 Formula 1 teams plus one supplier are live today. WEC/IMSA endurance racing is the next series being added. The rest are documented, not guessed at — see the source survey for exactly what's confirmed vs. still needs verification.
